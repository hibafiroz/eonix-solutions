> MongoDB Aggregation:

It is used to work with many documents and finally get one meaningful result. 
It works like a pipeline where documents goes step by step. Each step is called a stage.


How internally works:

Documents flow through each stage one by one, Every stage performs a specific operation. 
And the output of one stage automatically becomes the input for the next stage. 
Here the order of stages is very important. changing the order can completely change the final result and can affect performance.


> Core / Common Aggregation Stages in MongoDB:  starts with : {...}

1. $match
2. $project
3. $addFields
4. $group
5. $sort
6. $limit
7. $skip

> Advanced stages:

1. $lookup
2. $unwind
3. $count
4. $bucket
5. $facet
6. $unwind

Aggregation is mainly used for reports and analytics.
Example: Count users by city, Find total sales, Find average marks
Compared to find(), aggregation is more powerful because it can change and calculate data, not just fetch it.
